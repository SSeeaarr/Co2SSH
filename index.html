About

Homelessness is an issue that impacts many communities of the commonwealth. According to the Department of Community & Economic Development, they state that “On any given day, over 12,000 Pennsylvanians are known to be homeless…” (DCED, 2026).

The Coalition to Shelter & Support the Homeless (CSSH) is a volunteer-driven nonprofit organization based in Doylestown, Pennsylvania. Our mission is to provide emergency shelter, essential resources, and compassionate support to individuals experiencing homelessness in Bucks County and the surrounding communities.

CSSH operates a Code Blue emergency shelter that opens its doors when temperatures drop to 28 °F or below, during severe weather events, and on Wednesday planned-open nights. On any given night the shelter is open, we serve roughly 15–25 guests, providing a warm, safe place to sleep along with hot meals, clothing, hygiene products, and other necessities.

Beyond immediate shelter, we work to address the root causes of homelessness by connecting guests with resources that support long-term stability. From helping individuals obtain vital identification documents to providing financial assistance for housing move-in costs, our goal is to empower every person we serve on their path toward self-sufficiency.

None of this would be possible without our dedicated team of volunteers and community partners. Whether it is staffing overnight shifts, preparing meals, coordinating transportation, or organizing donation drives, our volunteers are the backbone of everything we do. We believe that through community action and compassion, we can create lasting change for our most vulnerable neighbors.

Services

Meals

Hot meals are provided to all shelter guests on nights the shelter is open. Dinner is served in the evening and a light breakfast is available in the morning.

A Shelter to Sleep in

A warm, safe place to sleep when temperatures drop to 28 °F or below, during severe weather events, and on Wednesday planned-open nights. Cots, blankets, and basic toiletries are provided.

Clothing

Donated clothing and seasonal outerwear are available at no cost. Items include coats, hats, gloves, socks, and other essentials to help guests stay warm and comfortable.

Medical Necessities

Access to basic medical supplies, hygiene products, and referrals to local healthcare providers. Staff can help connect guests with community health resources and follow-up care.

Financial Support to Access Housing

Assistance with security deposits, first-month rent, and other move-in costs to help individuals transition from homelessness into stable housing. Eligibility is assessed on a case-by-case basis.

Secure Needed Identification

Help obtaining vital documents such as state IDs, birth certificates, and Social Security cards, which are often required to access employment, housing, and other support programs.

FAQ

Q: How can I volunteer?
A: We welcome volunteers of all backgrounds and skill sets! You can sign up to volunteer by contacting Megan at codeblue@co2ssh.org

Q: How many guests utilize the shelter?
A: The shelter houses roughly 15-25 people on any night on which the shelter is open.

Q: How can I receive services?
A: If you or someone you know is in need of our services, please contact us at the phone number or email address listed in the Contact section below. We will work with you to understand your needs and connect you with the appropriate resources.

Q: What is the criteria to decide when to open the shelter?
A: CSSH opens the shelter when the weather is predicted to be 28 degrees or colder due to temperature or wind chill and/or if severe weather is predicted. Also, Wednesday nights are planned open nights. All open nights require full volunteer rosters.

Q: What happens if there are not enough volunteers to open the shelter?
A: If the volunteer slots are not filled by sunday at 9pm for Mon-Thurs shifts and Thursday by 9pm for Friday-Sunday shifts, then the shelter will not be able to be opened.


Requirements

CSSH does not have strict requirements to qualify for shelter services.
No documentation or identification is needed to receive shelter. However, all guests must follow a few basic rules while staying at the shelter: no smoking after lights out (10 PM), and treat everyone, volunteers and fellow guests alike, with respect.

How to Access Services

Call the CSSH Outreach
The CSSH Outreach team can be reached at (866) 390-0291, Ext. 703. They are available to provide information about our services, assess individual needs, and connect you with the appropriate resources. These teams operate 24 hours a day to find people in need and transport them to safe, heated, indoor locations.
